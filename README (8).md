## The Three Projects

### 01 — Smart Garage Predictive Maintenance
An AI-powered feature layer inside a myQ-style mobile app that catches garage door failures before they happen. Uses existing hardware telemetry — motor current draw, vibration signature, open/close timing — to build a device health score and alert users at the right moment. Connects directly to a vetted service marketplace so the alert converts to revenue, not just awareness.

What it shows: connected hardware + software product thinking, customer-insight-driven roadmap, cross-functional coordination across firmware, mobile, and data teams.

### 02 — Smart Access Pricing & Bundling Strategy
A four-tier pricing architecture — Basic hardware, Secure Home bundle, Pro Automation subscription, and Enterprise fleet — designed to capture value at every customer willingness-to-pay level. Includes bundle logic, gross margin rationale per tier, go-to-market sequencing, competitive benchmarking approach, and a structured A/B pricing experiment plan.

What it shows: P&L thinking, monetization strategy, product portfolio optimization, business impact mindset.

### 03 — Unified Smart Access Dashboard
A platform UX that consolidates garage, gate, camera, smart lock, and delivery access into a single interface. Covers the full product surface: device status, real-time activity timeline, guest access management, delivery permissions, and usage analytics. Includes a phased roadmap and a stakeholder alignment plan that names the specific cross-team coordination required to actually ship this.

What it shows: UX depth, platform thinking, ecosystem strategy, connected experience ownership.

## Design Notes

The visual design uses:
- **DM Serif Display** for headings — editorial weight, warm and confident
- **DM Sans** for body text — clean, readable at small sizes
- **IBM Plex Mono** for labels and metadata — technical precision where it matters
- A cream and charcoal palette with an orange accent that keeps things grounded and professional without looking like every other PM portfolio

Alternating light and dark section backgrounds help delineate the three projects at a glance without needing extra navigation.

## Metrics Shown

All KPIs in the portfolio are illustrative targets modeled on reasonable assumptions for a connected home hardware business at scale. They're meant to show the right way to think about measurement — specific, tied to business outcomes, mix of leading and lagging indicators — not to be taken as projections.

## If You're a Recruiter

Each project section follows the same structure I'd use in a real product review:

1. Problem statement — what's broken and why it matters
2. Business rationale — why solving it is worth the investment
3. Product strategy — the conceptual approach, not just a feature list
4. Feature breakdown — what we actually build and how it works
5. Metrics — how we know if it worked
6. Visual artifacts — wireframes, dashboards, and flow maps

That structure is deliberate. It's how I'd walk a team through a product decision.
